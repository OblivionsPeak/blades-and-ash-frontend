01

The Mark

Two shear blades cross to form an A — the craft and the name in a single stroke. The handle rings ground it like a maker's tool; a thread of ash rises from the pivot.

Built as pure vector with no fixed detail, the mark holds from a 16px favicon to a painted window. Use it solo as an avatar, app icon, stamp, or embroidery — anywhere the full name won't fit.

01b

Ornate Emblem

A richer take — crossed shears with finger rings, a metallic gold gradient, ash wisps, and mirror-perfect filigree.

This is the vector answer to the Gemini concept: ornate enough for signage and framed prints, but every curve stays editable, recolorable, and razor-sharp at any size. The symmetry is exact (one half is drawn and mirrored), which the AI render can't guarantee. It won't carry photoreal 3D gloss — that's the one thing only a raster/image tool delivers — so the pairing is: this emblem for showpieces, the simple monogram for favicons and tiny avatars.
